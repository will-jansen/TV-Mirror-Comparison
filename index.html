Key Considerations for Your 75" TV

1. Screen Mirroring Method

Camera-based: (e.g., Nanoleaf 4D, Govee T2/T3 Lite) Uses a camera to capture screen colors. Generally easier to set up and works with any HDMI input.

HDMI Sync Box: (e.g., Philips Hue, Lytmi Fantasy 3) Requires an additional box that sits between your TV and media devices. Offers precise color matching and often supports high refresh rates/resolutions but only works with devices connected through the box.

2. LED Type & Density

Look for RGBIC (Individual Segment Control) or addressable LEDs for smoother color transitions and more dynamic effects. Higher LED density generally means a more continuous and vibrant light output.

3. Ecosystem & Smart Home Integration

Consider if you want the lights to integrate with your existing smart home setup (e.g., Google Home, Amazon Alexa, Apple HomeKit, Matter). Philips Hue is strong here, while Nanoleaf, Govee, and Lytmi have their own robust apps and varying levels of voice assistant support.

4. Installation & Aesthetics

Most light strips use adhesive backing. Ensure the strip length is appropriate for your 75" TV's perimeter. Consider how the camera (if applicable) or sync box will look mounted or placed near your TV.

5. Gaming & High Refresh Rate Support

For gamers or those with high-end TVs, HDMI sync box solutions like Lytmi Fantasy 3 and Philips Hue (with the Sync Box) can support higher refresh rates (e.g., 4K@120Hz) directly through their passthrough, which camera-based systems typically don't affect.
